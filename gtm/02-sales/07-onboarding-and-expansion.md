# Onboarding & Expansion (Customer Success)

*Retention and expansion are cheaper than acquisition. The accumulating audit history is the moat — make the customer feel it.*

---

## Activation: the first 7 days (the only thing that matters early)

**Definition of activated:** SDK integrated → real traffic flowing → first probe fired → first report generated.

| Day | Milestone | Founder action |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Signup / contract | Welcome email: API key, one-line snippet, 5-min quickstart, calendar link |
| 0–1 | SDK integrated | Proactively check ingest is arriving; offer 15-min pairing if not |
| 1–3 | First probes fired | Confirm probes + golden sampling working; explain the dashboard |
| 3–5 | First drift/bias signal surfaced | Personal note: "here's what we caught + what it means" |
| 5–7 | **First report generated** | Walk them through their own PDF; this is the value moment |

If a customer hasn't activated in 7 days, intervene personally. Activation predicts retention more than anything else.

## Onboarding email sequence (lifecycle)
1. **T+0 Welcome:** key + one line + quickstart + "reply if stuck."
2. **T+1 Integrated?** nudge with the snippet again + offer pairing.
3. **T+3 Your dashboard:** how to read drift timeline + categories.
4. **T+7 Your first report:** generate + interpret; "this is what you send a buyer/regulator."
5. **T+14 Set your baseline cadence:** explain re-baselining after intentional changes.
6. **T+30 Monthly report ready:** auto-report + "what changed this month."

## Ongoing success rhythm
- **Monthly auto-report** delivered (recurring felt value — the heartbeat of retention).
- **Drift alert moments** (roadmap: Slack/email) — every "we caught X for you" is a renewal reason.
- **Quarterly business review** (Enterprise): trends, new probe categories, upcoming audit prep, expansion fit.

## Expansion triggers (when to upsell)
| Signal | Expansion move |
|---|---|
| Adds a second model/use case | More models monitored → higher tier |
| Their enterprise customer demands evidence | White-label reports → Enterprise |
| Upcoming SOC2/ISO/AI Act audit | Custom probes + SLA + audit package |
| More team members in dashboard | More seats |
| Hits free/Pro usage ceiling | Natural tier upgrade prompt (honest, in-product) |

## Retention safeguards
- **Re-baseline guidance:** prompt customers to re-baseline after deliberate prompt/model changes so scores stay honest (prevents false-alarm churn).
- **No surprise bills:** usage alerts before limits; transparent metering (see `03-pricing-billing`).
- **Easy downgrade, not just upgrade:** trust > short-term revenue. A customer who downgrades cleanly comes back.

## Turning customers into growth
- **Case study** at the first real win (with consent) → feeds marketing.
- **Reference calls** for Enterprise prospects (design partners especially).
- **Referrals:** "know another team running LLMs in {domain}?" — offer a credit both ways.
- **Trust badge:** "Monitored by Verispect" badge for their site/security page (only if genuinely active) → distribution + social proof.

## Churn early-warning signals
Falling probe volume, no logins, missed monthly report opens, integration removed, champion leaves the company. Reach out the moment any appears.

## Success metrics
Activation rate (7-day), time-to-first-report, monthly active accounts, net revenue retention, expansion rate, reports generated/account/month (the north star).
