# LinkedIn Outreach

*Relationship-based, lower legal risk than cold email (especially in strict jurisdictions like Germany). Often the better first touch for Tier-1 EU accounts.*

---

## Principles
- Connect like a peer, not a vendor. Founder-to-founder / engineer-to-engineer.
- Give before you ask. Lead with insight or their content, not a pitch.
- Never auto-spam. Manual or lightly-assisted, personalised every time.
- Move to a real conversation fast; LinkedIn is the doorway, not the venue.

## Connection request notes (≤300 chars)

**To a CTO/founder:**
> Hi {First} — building Verispect (active bias/drift detection for production LLMs, EU AI Act focus). Saw {Company} runs LLM {use case} — that's exactly the surface we watch. Would value connecting with someone solving this for real.

**To a Head of AI / RAI:**
> Hi {First} — I work on continuous AI behaviour assurance (Art. 10/72 evidence). Following how teams operationalise AI Act monitoring vs just policy. Would be glad to connect and trade notes.

**To a DPO/legal:**
> Hi {First} — building a compliance-monitoring tool designed to *reduce* data risk (we never ingest PII). Interested in how DPOs are scoping AI sub-processors under the Act. Connect?

## Post-accept message (Day 1–2, NO pitch)
> Thanks for connecting, {First}. Genuine question, not a pitch: how is {Company} handling the "prove the model didn't drift / isn't biased" question right now — is that on your plate yet or still future-you's problem? Curious what teams in {their domain} are actually doing.

→ Their answer qualifies them and opens the door. If pain surfaces, *then* offer value (sample report / quick call).

## Value-first follow-up (after they reply)
> Makes sense. If useful, I can generate a free drift/bias snapshot against your model — one line, ~5 min, and we never see your data (only hashes + vectors). You'd get a 2-page report showing if/where it's drifting. No strings — want me to send setup?

## Soft CTA to call
> Want to see it live for 15 min? I'll show real drift detection + the EU AI Act report it produces. {scheduling link}

## Founder content cadence on LinkedIn (inbound engine)
Post 3–4×/week. This builds the credibility a compliance buyer needs before replying. Themes:
1. **Findings:** "We probed {model} 1,000× for hiring bias. Here's what moved." (charts, no client data)
2. **Education:** "EU AI Act Art. 72 in plain English: what 'post-market monitoring' actually requires."
3. **Build-in-public:** shipping updates, the privacy architecture, why we never see data.
4. **Hot takes (honest):** "Logging ≠ monitoring. Here's the difference and why it matters for the Act."
5. **Customer wins (with consent):** "How {Customer} passed an enterprise security review in a week."

CTA on posts: "Running LLMs in hiring/credit/insurance? Comment 'report' and I'll send a free bias snapshot." → captures inbound leads natively.

## Engagement strategy
- Comment substantively on posts by RAI leaders, AI Act commentators, target-company founders. Be the most useful comment, not a plug.
- Join + contribute to AI Act / Responsible AI / MLOps LinkedIn groups and Slack/Discords.
- DM people who engage with your content — warmest possible lead.

## Metrics
- Connection accept rate (target >35% with good personalisation).
- Reply rate to post-accept message (target >25%).
- Posts → inbound "report" comments → snapshots run.
- LinkedIn-sourced demos booked.
