# Target-List Playbook — Build 300+ in a Day

*The Day-0 bottleneck. No list = no sprint. This gets you 300 high-fit prospects fast and legally. Quality > quantity, but you need volume of quality.*

---

## Who exactly (the filter)
EU/EEA + Singapore + UAE (+ UK, broader GCC as Tier-2) companies, 15–200 staff, seed–Series B, using an LLM in a **high-risk decision**: hiring, credit/lending, insurance, legal review, (carefully) health triage. Contact = CTO / technical founder / Head of AI / DPO.

## Sources (work them top to bottom)

### 1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator (primary — best signal)
Filters: Geography = EU countries + Singapore + UAE · Industry = HR-tech / Financial Services / Insurance / Legal Tech / Computer Software · Headcount 11–200 · Title = "CTO", "Co-founder", "Head of AI", "VP Engineering", "ML Lead", "DPO", "Head of Compliance".
Boolean keyword: `("LLM" OR "GPT" OR "OpenAI" OR "generative AI") AND ("hiring" OR "screening" OR "credit" OR "underwriting" OR "claims" OR "contract")`.
→ Save searches, work the list, note the {use case} for personalization.

### 2. Crunchbase / Dealroom (budget + board-pressure signal)
Recently-funded (last 12 mo) EU AI startups in the target verticals. A raise = budget + a board asking about regulatory risk = perfect timing.

### 3. Accelerator / VC portfolios
YC, Entrepreneur First, Antler, Techstars, Seedcamp, Station F, plus EU AI-focused VC portfolio pages (e.g. Air Street, Speedinvest, LocalGlobe). Founders there are reachable + fast-moving.

### 4. GitHub (technical signal)
Orgs whose public repos depend on `openai` / `anthropic` / `langchain` SDKs, located in target geos. Strong "they run LLMs in prod" signal. Find the CTO via the org's people.

### 5. Product Hunt / job boards
PH launches in HR-tech/fintech/legal-tech this year. Job posts mentioning "LLM in production," "responsible AI," "AI Act" → the company is both a fit and signaling the pain.

### 6. Events & communities
Attendee/speaker lists of RAI / AI Act / HR-tech / fintech events; members of Responsible AI and MLOps LinkedIn/Slack/Discord groups.

### 7. AI Act radar (highest-intent)
Companies already posting about EU AI Act readiness, hiring a "Head of AI Governance/Responsible AI," or publishing RAI commitments — they have the pain *and* are looking. Top of the list.

## Finding the email (legally)
- Prefer **LinkedIn DM** (no email needed, relationship-based, EU-safe).
- For email: company website (team/contact), or a B2B finder (Hunter, Apollo, etc.) returning **business** addresses only. Verify deliverability. Document the legitimate-interest basis per contact.
- Never use scraped consumer data or buy sketchy lists. We're a compliance company — clean sourcing only.

## The capture sheet (one row per prospect)
`Company · Person · Title · Geo · Vertical · {LLM use case} · Source · LinkedIn URL · Email · Personalization note · Status · Last touch · Next step+date`
(This is the tracker's "Prospects" tab — `05-sprint-tracker.xlsx`.)

## Daily quota
- **Day 0:** build 300 (4–6 focused hours). Front-load — this is the fuel.
- **Daily:** add 50 fresh as you work through the list, so you never run dry.
- Tier them: **A** (high-risk vertical + recent raise + EU = hit first), **B** (good fit), **C** (Tier-2/backup).

## Personalization at speed (the trick)
For each prospect, write ONE true sentence about their product + LLM use case in the capture note. That sentence is your message's first line. If you can't write it in 30 seconds from their site/LinkedIn, they're not researched enough — skip or downgrade to C. This is what makes 100 touches/day land instead of bounce.

## Legal guardrail (non-negotiable)
GDPR legitimate-interest: role-relevant only, identified sender, easy opt-out, business contacts, documented basis, suppression honored forever. In strict jurisdictions (e.g. Germany) prefer LinkedIn over cold email. Quality targeting *is* the legal compliance — a relevant message to a relevant person is defensible; spray-and-pray isn't.
