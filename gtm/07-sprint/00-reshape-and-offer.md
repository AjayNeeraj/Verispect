# The Reshape — Verispect, Sharpened for the Deadline

*Founder + CEO + CFO + design + legal, one voice. The essence is unchanged. The packaging is now a weapon. Goal: make a busy EU AI founder say "yes" in one conversation.*

---

## 1. The reshape in one move

We stop selling **a cheap monitoring tool** (slow, "nice to have," a months-long close). We start selling **a deadline outcome at a premium price** — "Vanta for the EU AI Act":

> **"AI Act Readiness — proof your AI behaves, before 2 August 2026. Live in a day. We never see your data."**

Same product. Same code. The wedge is now an **outcome with a date on it**, not a feature with a price on it. People don't buy "drift detection." They buy *not being the company that fails the audit / loses the enterprise deal / makes the news*. The Act gives us the one thing every fast sale needs: **a real, external, non-negotiable deadline.**

Tagline for the sprint:
> **"The EU AI Act hits 2 Aug 2026. Is your AI ready to prove itself? Verispect makes it ready in a day."**

## 3. The Irresistible Offer — "Founding 20" Program

This is the close mechanism. Built so a yes is *easy, fast, safe, and urgent*.

**The Founding 20 — for the first 20 EU/EEA, Singapore & UAE AI companies who get audit-ready before the deadline.**

Each Founding Customer gets:
1. **AI Act Readiness Report** — the branded, article-mapped compliance PDF, generated on their real model. *(the artifact)*
2. **Continuous monitoring** — active bias/drift probes + monthly auto-report. *(the recurring value)*
3. **White-glove setup** — founder integrates the one line *with* them on a 30-min call. Zero effort. *(removes friction)*
4. **"Verispect Verified — Monitoring Active" trust badge** — for their site/security page. *(distribution + status — they market us)*
5. **Founding rate, locked for life** — **$1,500/mo (or $15,000/yr)**, locked before it **rises to $2,500/mo** after the Founding 20. Never increases for you. *(loss-aversion via the rise, not a discount — keeps the premium frame)*
6. **Direct founder line** — private Slack/WhatsApp with Ajay. *(white-glove, builds the reference relationship)*

**Scarcity:** only 20 spots. **Urgency:** the deadline + spots filling publicly ("7 of 20 left").
**Risk reversal:** *"Run the free snapshot first. See your real drift score in 5 minutes. Only join if it shows you something worth fixing. Cancel anytime — no lock-in."*

> Why this closes fast: low price (easy budget yes) + locked-for-life (act now or pay more later) + scarcity (20) + deadline (Aug 2) + zero effort (we set it up) + zero risk (free snapshot first, cancel anytime). Every psychological lever, all honest.

## 4. CFO math — does "Founding 20" hit the target?

Goal: 10–20 sales in 15 days.
- 20 founding customers on **annual prepay $15,000** = **$300,000 cash** in the door + $300k ARR locked.
- 20 on **monthly $1,500** = $30,000 MRR = $360,000 ARR.
- Even 10 closes = **$15k MRR / $180k ARR** — a real seed-stage base from one cohort.
- **Push annual** for the sprint (cash + commitment + lower churn). Offer monthly as the fallback for hesitant yeses.
- Blended realistic 15-day outcome (see battle plan): ~10–15 closes, mix of annual + monthly ≈ **€8k–14k cash + €12k–19k ARR**. Strong for a solo founder's first 15 days, and 20 reference logos that fuel everything after.

The founding discount costs us €20/mo/customer forever — cheap price for 20 referenceable logos, testimonials, and the case studies that make month 2 easy. **Customer acquisition, paid in margin not cash.** Correct CFO trade early.

## 5. Design direction (final designer hat)

One identity across snapshot → report → badge → site → deck. Purple `#7c6eff` on near-black `#0f1117`, clean technical sans, lots of whitespace, the compliance "ring" as the signature visual.
- **The badge** is the design priority — it must look *prestigious* (think "SOC 2" / "verified" energy) because customers display it; it's free advertising. Dark pill, purple check, "Verispect Verified · Monitoring Active."
- **The report** must look like something a lawyer/auditor respects (already does — the reportlab PDF). That's the credibility anchor; lead every demo with it on screen.
- **The campaign page** (file 04): one screen, one offer, one CTA, a live "X of 20 spots left" counter, the countdown to Aug 2.

## 6. Legal hat — keep it clean while we move fast

Speed must not break the trust brand. Hard rules for all sprint copy (from `04-legal-compliance/09`):
- Sell **"readiness" and "evidence,"** never **"compliant/certified/approved."** "Get audit-ready," not "get compliant."
- The badge says **"Monitoring Active,"** never "Compliant" — it claims a true fact (monitoring is running), not a legal verdict.
- Cite **2 Aug 2026** as the operative date; note the Digital Omnibus *may* move standalone Annex III to Dec 2027 if adopted — don't pretend the delay is certain, but the urgency is real either way (procurement asks now).
- Outbound stays GDPR legitimate-interest: role-relevant, identified, one-click opt-out, business contacts only. We are a compliance brand — our outreach is the demo.

## 7. The essence, kept

Unchanged: active assurance over passive logging; honest evidence over false certification; privacy by architecture. The reshape doesn't dilute any of it — it puts a **date, a price, and a reason to act today** around the same true thing. That's the whole job.
