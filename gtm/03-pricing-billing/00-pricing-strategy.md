# Pricing Strategy

*How we price, why, and the psychology behind each tier. Value-based, not cost-plus. The PDF report is the value crystallisation; pricing is anchored to "cost of failing an audit / losing a deal," not to "cost of logging."*

---

## Pricing philosophy

1. **Anchor to the alternative's pain, not our cost.** A stalled enterprise deal or a failed AI Act audit costs €10k–€millions. €99/mo is a rounding error against that. Never let the buyer compare us to a €79 logging tool — we're in the compliance-insurance category.
2. **Free tier = distribution + lead gen**, not charity. It must produce a *shareable artifact* (a real report) so it markets us.
3. **Land low, expand on value.** Most accounts start Free or Pro self-serve; the money is in the Pro→Enterprise graduation driven by their own enterprise customers/audits.
4. **Transparent + honest.** Published prices for Free/Pro. No dark patterns, easy cancellation, usage alerts before limits. (We're a trust brand — pricing must reflect it.)

## Why each tier exists (the ladder logic)

- **Free** — removes all friction to the "aha." Produces one watermarked report = a marketing asset they share with their boss. Converts on usage ceiling + the desire to remove the watermark + needing >1 report.
- **Pro (€99)** — the self-serve workhorse. Priced as an obvious yes for a funded startup (< 1 hr of eng time/mo). One use case, real monitoring, real reports.
- **Business (€399)** — the bridge. For teams whose *own* customers now demand evidence: more models, unlimited reports, longer retention. This is where serious money starts and where most expansion lands.
- **Enterprise (custom)** — the audit-bound, multi-team, white-label, SLA buyers. Sales-led. White-label reports + custom probes + audit support = high willingness to pay; anchored to "cost of failing conformity."

## Price anchoring in sales
Present Enterprise first when relevant, then Business, so €399 feels reasonable and €99 feels trivial. For self-serve, default the page to Pro highlighted ("Most popular").

## Annual discount
~2 months free on annual (€990 vs €1,188; €3,990 vs €4,788). Improves cash + retention. Push annual at renewal, not aggressively at first signup (trust first).

## Add-ons (à la carte, mostly Enterprise)
- Additional custom probe set: from €500 one-off / €150/mo maintained.
- White-label / co-branded reports: included Enterprise; €99/mo add-on for Business.
- Extra retention beyond tier: priced per data volume.
- Audit-support engagement (auditor liaison, evidence dossier): from €2,500 per audit cycle.

## What we will NOT do
- No per-seat gouging that punishes adoption (seats are generous; we monetise value/scale, not headcount).
- No charging for the DPA or basic privacy features — privacy is the brand, never a paywall.
- No hidden overage shock — always alert before limits and let them choose.

## Future pricing levers (revisit at scale)
- Usage-based metering on probe volume for very high-traffic accounts.
- Per-report pricing for occasional/audit-only buyers (a "one report" SKU for non-subscribers facing a single audit).
- Channel/OEM pricing for GRC platforms and audit firms embedding Verispect.

## Grandfathering
Early customers and design partners keep their pricing on any future increase (and we say so — it builds loyalty and urgency to buy now).
